Exploring Northern Michigan

Welcome to the heartland of the Midwest, where the Great Lakes meet diverse landscapes and friendly communities â€“ Michigan. From the urban vibrancy of Detroit to the serene shores of Lake Michigan, the state offers a multifaceted travel experience. Join me on a journey as we explore the gems that make Michigan a destination worth discovering. Although living here for around 2 years now, I never explored outside of Ann Arbor. This summer, I visited up north with my family and cousins, and got to learn more about nature. Here are some things that we did and more about them!

Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore:
Traverse the state and find yourself on the shores of Lake Michigan at Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore. Unwind on pristine beaches, marvel at towering sand dunes, and savor panoramic views of the lake. Hike the trails, go bird watching, or simply relish the beauty of this natural oasis.

Mackinac Island:
Step back in time on Mackinac Island, a charming retreat where horse-drawn carriages replace cars. Explore Victorian architecture, bike along scenic paths, and indulge in world-famous fudge. With no motor vehicles allowed, Mackinac Island offers a peaceful escape surrounded by the beauty of Lake Huron.

Grand Rapids Art Museum:
Art enthusiasts will find solace in Grand Rapids, home to the Grand Rapids Art Museum. Immerse yourself in a diverse collection of visual arts, from contemporary masterpieces to thought-provoking exhibitions. Grand Rapids' commitment to public art and cultural enrichment is evident throughout the city.

Boyne Mountain:
In the summer, Boyne Mountain transforms into a captivating haven for outdoor enthusiasts, offering a vibrant tapestry of recreational activities against the backdrop of Michigan's lush landscapes. The slopes that once echoed with the laughter of winter sports enthusiasts now beckon hikers and mountain bikers to explore miles of trails, each unveiling panoramic views of rolling hills and dense woodlands.
